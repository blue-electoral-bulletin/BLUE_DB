Special units are generated by resolver rules in
\texttt{municipality\_resolver.py}.  In practice, code patterns vary by
country and by special-unit type.  The most recurrent conventions are:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{CC\_CCYY99}: country-level postal or residual aggregate
      (e.g.\ \texttt{LT\_LTYY99}, \texttt{EE\_EEYY99}, \texttt{RO\_ROYY99}).
  \item \texttt{CC\_CCYY98}: country-level non-postal aggregate where needed
      (e.g.\ national aggregate rows).
  \item \texttt{CC\_CCZZXX}: foreign-voter rows; \texttt{XX} is usually an
      ISO-like host-country suffix (e.g.\ \texttt{LV\_LVZZDE}).
  \item Country-specific district sentinels (e.g.\ Greek electoral districts,
      Croatian district codes, Belgian canton codes, German \texttt{...999}
      brief-vote aggregates).
  \item \texttt{PL\_PLZZ98}: dedicated ships-at-sea aggregate
      (\textit{Statki}).
\end{itemize}

The controlled type vocabulary used in \texttt{geo/special.csv} is
summarised in Table~\ref{tab:special_types}.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lp{10cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{Type} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
Postal Vote              & Votes cast by post, typically not attributable to
                           a specific commune.  Usually aggregated at the
                           district or country level. \\
Abroad Voters            & Votes cast by citizens residing abroad.  May be
                           further broken down by country of residence. \\
Electoral Constituency   & An electoral district that does not correspond to

                           code due to boundary changes, data anomalies, or
                           special administrative status. \\

                           dedicated aggregate (currently used in Poland). \\

\bottomrule

\paragraph{Overseas territories and OCT coding.}
French and Dutch overseas units are represented with dedicated FR/NL special
entries and extra parent references (e.g.\ \texttt{FRY50}, \texttt{FRZ10}–
\texttt{FRZ50}, \texttt{NL901}–\texttt{NL903}) to preserve territory-level
aggregation in election outputs.
