Two aggregate types share the feature that votes cannot be attributed to a
specific commune and are grouped into a dedicated row using identifiers in the
\texttt{CC\_CCYYxx} range.

\paragraph{Postal votes.}
Votes cast by post are recorded at district or national level and are assigned
special identifiers.
Postal-vote rows are present for Austria, Germany, Hungary, Lithuania, Latvia,
and the Netherlands.
Where a country uses official postal-vote district codes (as is the case in
Austria and Germany), those codes are retained, and
each postal-vote row is linked to its proper NUTS~3 parent unit wherever
possible. Otherwise, special identifiers of the form \texttt{CC\_CCYYxx} are used.

\paragraph{E-Voting.}
Electronic votes cast in some Estonian elections are aggregated into a single
national row (\texttt{EE\_EEYY99}).
